KEY CONCEPTS

Benign Smooth Muscle Tumors

  • Leiomyomas are the most common uterine tumor; variants include cellular, epithelioid, bizarre, lipoleiomyoma, mitotically active (in women <35), intravenous leiomyomatosis, and benign metastasizing leiomyoma
  • Hormonally responsive (most shrink after menopause)

Malignant Smooth Muscle Tumors

  • Leiomyosarcomas characterized by hypercellularity, cytologic atypia, coagulative necrosis, and mitotic activity >10/10 hpf
  • Large tumors (>10 cm) with coagulative necrosis and >10 mitoses/10 hpf have dismal prognosis (<5 years survival)

STUMP Criteria

  • High mitotic activity (>5-10/10 hpf) but lacking significant cytologic atypia and necrosis
  • Atypical epithelioid, bizarre, and IVL variants also fall into uncertain malignant potential category

Endometrial Stromal Tumors

  • Distinguished by CD10 positivity, evenly spaced vessels resembling spiral arterioles, and characteristic translocations
  • Low-grade ESS: JAZF1-SUZ12 fusion (t(7;17)); shows lymphovascular invasion with wormlike plugs
  • High-grade ESS: YWHAE-FAM22 fusion (t(10;17)), ZC3H7B-BCOR fusion, or BCOR ITD
  • Mitotic activity NOT predictive of aggressive behavior in low-grade ESS
  • Cytologic atypia more accurate than mitotic count in distinguishing low-grade from high-grade and predicting behavior

Adenomyosis

  • Diagnosis requires endometrial glands >2-2.5 mm from basalis in properly oriented sections
  • Distinguish from tangentially cut normal endometrium by examining deeper levels
