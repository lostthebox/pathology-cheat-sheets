ACUTE ENDOMETRITIS (AR) Associated with PID; may be associated with pregnancy; elevated temperature, leukocytosis, discomfort, pain When secondary to PID: tubo-ovarian complex with fibrous adhesions, fibrinous exudate, congestion, edema; associated with IUD use Numerous neutrophils in stroma AND glands; ectatic blood vessels; fibrin exudate; hemorrhage Tissue Gram stain to identify microorganisms Menstruation: vacuoles in distended glands, decidual change in stroma Clinical history important, particularly as it relates to menstrual cycle; aseptic abortion is a possibility; patient may present with acute abdomen
CHRONIC ENDOMETRITIS (AR) Common; may be asymptomatic or pelvic pain; associated with IUD, PID Nonspecific PLASMA CELLS in stroma (diagnostic); difficult to date; focal crowding mimicking hyperplasia None Hyperplasia: plasma cells unusual; Adenocarcinoma: cribriform/confluent architecture Plasma cells REQUIRED for diagnosis (lymphocytes/neutrophils normally present); diagnosis of hyperplasia with caution if endometritis present
ENDOMETRIAL POLYP (C) Common in middle-aged/postmenopausal women; abnormal bleeding; tamoxifen effect Usually solitary, variable size; arises in fundus; pedunculated or sessile Irregularly outlined glands out of phase; fibrovascular stalk with thick-walled vessels; metaplastic epithelium common None Hyperplasia: diffuse process; Polypoid adenoCA: back-to-back glands, no stroma; Adenosarcoma: atypical stroma Arises from basalis; <0.5% show focal adenoCA confined to polyp; hyperplasia may be confined to polyp; chromosome 6 inversion
ENDOMETRIAL HYPERPLASIA (C) Middle-aged to postmenopausal; abnormal bleeding; unopposed estrogen (obesity, nulliparity, estrogen-producing tumors); PTEN inactivation Thickened endometrium; may appear polypoid or normal with velvety surface Increased gland:stroma ratio; Simple = crowded round/dilated glands; Complex = irregular branching; Atypia = nuclear enlargement, hyperchromasia, prominent nucleoli None; atypical hyperplasia more likely aneuploid Proliferative endometrium: even gland distribution; Polyp: focal, thick-walled vessels; Adenocarcinoma: cribriform, back-to-back glands Atypia vs no atypia more clinically relevant than simple vs complex; atypical hyperplasia = precursor to endometrioid adenoCA; coexists with CA in 25-40%; may respond to progestin
SEROUS ENDOMETRIAL INTRAEPITHELIAL CARCINOMA (SEIC) (C) Precursor of serous carcinoma; postmenopausal, median age 60; also called "carcinoma in situ" None visible Markedly atypical nuclei with prominent nucleoli identical to invasive serous CA; lines surface/polyps/glands; papillary contour; adjacent endometrium atrophic p53+; high Ki-67 Serous CA: desmoplasia, confluent pattern, >10mm; Eosinophilic metaplasia: smudged nuclei; Tubal metaplasia: mixed cell types Potential to metastasize widely even WITHOUT stromal invasion; NOT associated with endometrioid CA; lacks desmoplasia/confluent pattern
ENDOMETRIOID ADENOCARCINOMA (C) Type I: obesity, nulliparity, late menopause, unopposed estrogen; preceded by hyperplasia; abnormal bleeding Uterus enlarged; single dominant mass, soft, tan-white, friable; endometrium diffusely thickened Most common type; crowded complex branching glands with cribriforming/papillary/solid/back-to-back; FIGO grading: Grade 1 <5% solid, Grade 2 6-50% solid, Grade 3 >50% solid CK+, vimentin often+, CEA often-, ER/PR+ Hyperplasia: stroma between glands; Serous CA: p16+, p53+; Metastatic adenoCA: serosal invasion, history Squamous differentiation NOT prognostically significant; depth of myometrial invasion important prognostic parameter; villoglandular type low grade
SEROUS CARCINOMA (C) Type II: NOT estrogen-dependent; preceded by atrophic endometrium; postmenopausal, age 60s+; abnormal bleeding Exophytic tan-pink tumor; superficial myometrial invasion Papillary structures with complex branching; marked nuclear pleomorphism, prominent nucleoli; hobnail cells; psammoma bodies (30%) p16 diffuse+, p53 strong+ or null; WT1 may be+; ER/PR usually- Endometrioid: less atypia, ER/PR+; Clear cell: clear cytoplasm, napsin A+ By definition HIGH GRADE; poor prognosis; often presents with extrauterine disease; diffuse p16 and aberrant p53 diagnostic
CLEAR CELL CARCINOMA (F) Usually preceded by atrophic endometrium; women in 60s+; abnormal bleeding Similar to endometrioid Papillary, glandular, or solid architecture; cells with abundant clear cytoplasm filled with glycogen; pleomorphic nuclei; hobnail cells (nuclei protrude into lumens) PAS+ (diastase sensitive); ER/PR-, p53-, napsin A+ Serous: p53+, napsin A-; Endometrioid: less clear cytoplasm By definition HIGH GRADE; poor prognosis; glycogen in cytoplasm PAS+ but diastase sensitive (vs mucinous)
ATYPICAL POLYPOID ADENOMYOMA (AR) Late reproductive years; abnormal bleeding; benign clinical course; curettage may be curative Solitary polypoid mass in lower uterine segment Random glands with irregular outlines in SMOOTH MUSCLE stroma (short fascicles, few mitoses); atypical epithelium with loss of polarity but no outright pleomorphism; squamous metaplasia/morules common p16 stromal expression helps DDx Hyperplasia: diffuse, no smooth muscle; Infiltrating CA: desmoplasia, cribriforming; Carcinosarcoma: malignant spindle component Benign despite atypical appearance; distinguished by short smooth muscle bundles; curettage alone may be curative
ADENOFIBROMA Extremely rare and benign; perimenopausal/postmenopausal; abnormal bleeding Lobulated polyploid, variable size; tan-brown, hemorrhagic; numerous small cysts on cut surface Benign stroma and glands; <4 mitotic figures/10 HPF; cystic spaces and papillary projections; unremarkable cuboidal/columnar epithelium None Adenosarcoma: atypical stromal cells, mitotic index >5/10 HPF Hysterectomy to prevent recurrence and exclude adenosarcoma
ADENOSARCOMA (AR) Any age; abnormal bleeding; arises in endometrium (rarely cervix) Solitary sessile polypoid mass filling cavity; tan-gray with small cysts, hemorrhage/necrosis LEAFLIKE pattern (phyllodes-like); benign epithelium, malignant stroma; periglandular stromal cellularity; stroma atypical with 4-20 mitoses/10 HPF; homologous (fibrosarcoma) or heterologous (rhabdomyosarcoma, chondrosarcoma) CK+ epithelium; vimentin+ stroma; desmin/MSA+ if rhabdomyosarcoma; high MIB-1 around glands Adenofibroma: <4 mitoses/10 HPF, bland stroma; Carcinosarcoma: malignant epithelium; Endometrial stromal sarcoma: no leaflike pattern Benign epithelium + malignant stroma; periepithelial stromal cellularity characteristic; 20% myometrial invasion; 25-40% recur, 5% metastasize (sarcoma only); sarcomatous overgrowth (â‰¥25%) = worse prognosis
CARCINOSARCOMA (AR) Postmenopausal; often history of pelvic radiation; abnormal bleeding, pain; bulky tumors protruding through cervical os Large friable polypoid mass filling cavity, invading myometrium; variegated with hemorrhage/necrosis; hard foci if heterologous elements (bone/cartilage) MALIGNANT glands (any type adenoCA) + MALIGNANT spindle cells; homologous (fibrosarcoma, leiomyosarcoma) or heterologous (rhabdomyosarcoma most common, chondrosarcoma, osteosarcoma); hybrid features Vimentin+ both components; CK 8/18+ epithelium, focally+ spindle; desmin/myoglobin+ rhabdomyosarcoma; S-100+ chondro/liposarcoma; CD10+ stromal sarcoma Poorly diff endometrioid CA: lacks biphasic cells; Adenosarcoma: benign epithelium Also called malignant mixed mesodermal tumor (MMMT); early metastases = epithelial; late metastases = sarcomatous overgrowth; lungs common metastatic site; poor prognosis
ADENOMYOSIS (C) Nonneoplastic; common in adult women; enlarged uterus, abnormal bleeding, dysmenorrhea; often with leiomyomas; often incidental Thickened myometrium with focal soft discolored areas or small cysts Endometrial glands + endometrial stroma within myometrium >2-2.5mm from basalis; glands often inactive, hemosiderin absent CD10+ confirms endometrial stroma (vs smooth muscle) Tangential section: deeper levels show continuity; CA involving adenomyosis: malignant glands with stroma, not myometrial invasion; Invasive adenoCA: desmoplasia Properly oriented section critical (tangential sections mimic adenomyosis); >2-2.5mm from basalis required
LEIOMYOMA (C) Benign "fibroid"; most common tumor in women and uterus; rare <20y, increasingly common >30y; may cause pain, dysmenorrhea, urinary/bowel changes, infertility Often multiple; discrete, well-circumscribed, firm, whorled, tan-white; cystic degeneration in larger tumors; 3 types: subserosal, intramural, submucosal Interlacing fascicles of bland monomorphic spindle (smooth muscle) cells; well-circumscribed; <5 mitoses/10 HPF; pseudocapsule; hyalinization/calcifications may be present Desmin+, SMA+, h-caldesmon+ Leiomyosarcoma: >10 mitoses/10 HPF, atypia, necrosis; Cellular leiomyoma: increased cellularity but <5 mitoses; Atypical leiomyoma: atypia but <5 mitoses Benign; treatment often observation or hysterectomy; variants include cellular, atypical, mitotically active, lipoleiomyoma

KEY CONCEPTS

CRITICAL IHC PANELS

  • Endometrioid Adenocarcinoma: CK+, vimentin often+, ER/PR+ (useful to distinguish from endocervical adenoCA which is vimentin-, CEA+)
  • Serous Carcinoma: p16 diffuse+, p53 strong+ or null, WT1 may be+, ER/PR usually-
  • Clear Cell Carcinoma: PAS+ (diastase sensitive), napsin A+, ER/PR-, p53-
  • Adenomyosis: CD10+ confirms endometrial stroma around glands
  • Smooth Muscle: Desmin+, SMA+, h-caldesmon+

TYPE I VS TYPE II CARCINOMA

  • Type I (Endometrioid): Estrogen-dependent, preceded by hyperplasia, obesity/nulliparity/unopposed estrogen, ER/PR+, better prognosis
  • Type II (Serous, Clear Cell): NOT estrogen-dependent, preceded by atrophic endometrium, older age, p53 mutations, ER/PR-, poor prognosis

ENDOMETRIAL HYPERPLASIA CLASSIFICATION

  • Architecture: Simple (round/dilated glands) vs Complex (irregular branching)
  • Cytology: Without Atypia vs With Atypia (MORE clinically relevant)
  • Clinical Significance: Atypical hyperplasia = precursor to endometrioid adenoCA; coexists with CA in 25-40%
  • EIN terminology: Occasionally used but NOT commonly adopted

FIGO GRADING (ENDOMETRIOID ADENOCARCINOMA)

  • Grade 1: <5% solid (non-squamous, non-morular) areas
  • Grade 2: 6-50% solid areas
  • Grade 3: >50% solid areas
  • Note: Prominent nuclear atypia should raise grade by 1; serous and clear cell are by definition high grade

ENDOMETRITIS

  • Acute endometritis: Neutrophils in stroma AND glands; associated with PID, pregnancy; may present with acute abdomen
  • Chronic endometritis: Plasma cells REQUIRED (lymphocytes/neutrophils normally present); associated with IUD, PID
  • Diagnosis of hyperplasia with caution if endometritis present

DIAGNOSTIC PITFALLS

  • Adenomyosis: Requires properly oriented section; glands >2-2.5mm from basalis; tangential sections can mimic it
  • CA involving adenomyosis: NOT considered myometrial invasion (still surrounded by endometrial stroma)
  • Atypical polypoid adenomyoma: Benign despite atypical appearance; smooth muscle stroma is key
  • SEIC: Can metastasize widely even WITHOUT stromal invasion

HIGH-YIELD MORPHOLOGY

  • Leaflike/Phyllodes pattern: Adenosarcoma
  • Hobnail cells: Serous carcinoma, Clear cell carcinoma (nuclei protrude into gland lumens)
  • Psammoma bodies: Serous carcinoma (30% of cases)
  • Thick-walled vessels: Endometrial polyp (diagnostic feature)
  • Squamous morules: Atypical polypoid adenomyoma (very common)

BENIGN VS MALIGNANT MESENCHYMAL

  • Adenofibroma: Benign stroma, <4 mitoses/10 HPF
  • Adenosarcoma: Benign epithelium + malignant stroma, 4-20 mitoses/10 HPF, periepithelial cellularity
  • Carcinosarcoma: Malignant epithelium + malignant stroma (both components malignant)
  • Leiomyoma: Bland smooth muscle, <5 mitoses/10 HPF

MITOTIC RATE CUTOFFS

  • <4 mitoses/10 HPF: Adenofibroma (benign)
  • 4-20 mitoses/10 HPF: Adenosarcoma (malignant stroma)
  • <5 mitoses/10 HPF: Leiomyoma (benign smooth muscle)
