KEY CONCEPTS

TYPE I VS TYPE II OVARIAN CARCINOMAS

  • Type I (Low-Grade): Stepwise progression cystadenoma to borderline to carcinoma; stage 1 at presentation; slow, indolent; K-ras/BRAF mutations 65%, TP53 8%; includes low-grade serous, mucinous, endometrioid, clear cell, Brenner
  • Type II (High-Grade): De novo; high stage at presentation; rapidly growing, aggressive; p53 mutation 70%, K-ras/BRAF rare 1%; includes high-grade serous, carcinosarcoma

CRITICAL IHC PANELS

  • High-Grade Serous: p53 strong+ or null (diagnostic), WT-1+, CA-125+, vimentin+, PAX8+
  • Mucinous: CK7+, CK20+, CEA+, vimentin-
  • Endometrioid: Vimentin+ (KEY), ER/PR+, WT-1-
  • Clear Cell: Napsin A+, PAS+ (glycogen), HNF-1β+, ER/PR-, p53-
  • Brenner: CK8/18+, CK20-, GATA3+, p63+, uroplakin+, WT1-
  • AGCT: Inhibin+, calretinin+, CD56+, EMA-, CK7-; FOXL2 C402G >90%
  • SLCT: Inhibin+, calretinin+, vimentin+; DICER1 >60%
  • SCTAT: Inhibin+ focally; STK11 (PJS)
  • Gynandroblastoma: None contributory; DICER1 p.E1705K ~40%
  • Dysgerminoma: PLAP+, Oct4+, Sall4+, c-kit+; CK-, AFP-
  • YST: AFP+ (diagnostic), glypican-3+, Sall4+, CK+
  • Embryonal: CD30+, Oct4+, Sall4+, CK+, PLAP+; EMA-
  • Choriocarcinoma: HCG+, HPL+, inhibin+ (ST)
  • Gonadoblastoma: Germ (Oct4+, Sall4+, PLAP+, CD117+), Sex cord (inhibin+, calretinin+); Y/TSPY1

TUMOR MARKERS

  • CA-125: High-grade serous (not specific)
  • CEA, CA 19-9: Mucinous
  • AFP: YST (diagnostic), rarely embryonal
  • HCG: Choriocarcinoma (very high), embryonal, dysgerminoma (if ST)
  • LDH: Dysgerminoma
  • Inhibin: Sex cord-stromal, some mucinous

SEROUS CARCINOMA ORIGIN

  • Majority high-grade serous from fallopian tube fimbria, not ovary
  • STIC in tube = fallopian tube primary
  • Surface serous have large hemorrhagic papillary excrescences on ovarian surface

CHARACTERISTIC FEATURES

  • Call-Exner bodies: AGCT (small round cystic spaces with eosinophilic material/pyknotic nuclei)
  • Grooved nuclei: AGCT, Brenner
  • Schiller-Duval bodies: YST (pathognomonic; papillae with central vessel)
  • Hobnail cells: Clear cell (nuclei protrude into lumens)
  • Psammoma bodies: Serous, endosalpingiosis
  • Rokitansky nodule: Mature cystic teratoma (solid with bone/teeth)
  • Picket fence: Mucinous (columnar, apical mucin, basal nuclei)
  • Lymphocytic stroma: Dysgerminoma
  • Annular tubules: SCTAT (encircling hyaline)
  • Reinke crystals: Leydig cell (eosinophilic crystalloids)
  • Pseudolobules with vessels: Sclerosing stromal

ENDOMETRIOSIS-ASSOCIATED

  • Clear cell and endometrioid most common in endometriosis
  • Endometrioma risk factor for malignant transformation (~0.5%)

BILATERALITY

  • High (>50%): High-grade serous 65%
  • Moderate (10-20%): Serous cystadenoma 10%, mature cystic teratoma 10-15%, mucinous CA <20%, Brenner malignant 15%
  • Low (<5%): Mucinous cystadenoma 2-4%
  • Usually unilateral: Dysgerminoma, YST, embryonal, immature teratoma, AGCT, thecoma, fibroma (bilateral in dysgenesis for dysgerminoma); gonadoblastoma frequently bilateral

AGE DISTRIBUTIONS

  • Children/Young (1st-2nd decades): Immature teratoma, YST, embryonal, dysgerminoma, SLCT
  • Reproductive (<30y): Mature cystic teratoma (most common <30y)
  • Middle-aged (4th-5th decades): Serous cystadenoma, mucinous tumors, fibroma, AGCT, endometrioid, clear cell
  • Postmenopausal (6th-7th decades): High-grade serous 40-70y, thecoma, mucinous CA, Brenner malignant, carcinosarcoma

PROGNOSIS

  • Excellent: Mature cystic teratoma, serous cystadenoma, mucinous cystadenoma, fibroma, thecoma
  • Good with treatment: Dysgerminoma (chemo-sensitive), immature teratoma (low grade)
  • Intermediate: Borderline low stage, mucinous CA 40% 5-year
  • Poor: High-grade serous (high stage at presentation), carcinosarcoma, clear cell, YST, embryonal, choriocarcinoma, Brenner malignant 35% 5-year
